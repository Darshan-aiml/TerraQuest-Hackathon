What is TerraQuest?

TerraQuest is a 24-hour hackathon by the Department of AI/ML, Sri Sairam College of Engineering, focused on building impactful solutions for a sustainable future.

This year’s theme is the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), challenging participants to create projects that tackle real-world issues — from climate action and clean energy to quality education and sustainable cities.

Join us for 24 hours of coding, learning, and collaboration, where bold ideas turn into solutions that drive global well-being.

Choose from the example problem statements below, or Propose your own problem within these SDGs.

Water → SDG 6

Safe water and proper sanitation are vital for communities and ecosystems.

Fire → SDG 7

Fire represents energy, and the goal is to shift towards renewable and clean energy sources.

Sky → SDG 13

The sky symbolizes our atmosphere, highlighting the need to fight climate change and protect the planet.

Land → SDG 15

Healthy land ecosystems support biodiversity, forests, and sustainable life on Earth.

FAQ's

Terraquest is a hackathon event where participants collaborate to solve real world challenges and build innovative solutions within a limited timeframe under sustainable development goals.

Anyone interested in technology, innovation, and problem-solving can participate. Students, professionals, and enthusiasts are all welcome.

You can register through our official website by filling out the registration form and following the instructions provided.

₹500 per person.

Prizes include cash rewards, goodies, T-shirt.

You can use any technology stack you are comfortable with. We encourage creativity and innovation in your solutions.

Follow us on our social media channels and join the WhatsApp group for the latest updates and announcements.

A team can have up to 3-4 members.

You have two options: Work on a problem statements of your choice from the given theme. Select from the problem statements provided in the brochure. You're free to choose either option based on your preference.
